Who we are — A student studio with practical focus

AyTech Studio grew out of the simple idea that real learning happens when you build real things. As a Computer Science & Engineering student, I balance college with side projects and freelance work, and I founded this studio to document my journey, help small clients, and craft useful tools for students and startups.

Background & Vision

In college, I noticed many classmates had great ideas but no one to quickly prototype them. AyTech Studio started as a response — a small, focused studio that can build MVPs, websites and apps at student-friendly rates while maintaining a professional approach.

The vision is simple: create reliable, maintainable products while documenting the learning process. I prefer small cross-disciplinary projects: a mix of web engineering, mobile UX, and lightweight AI tools that actually help users.

Approach & Values

  • Build fast prototypes, iterate often.
  • Design with clarity — simple interfaces beat flashy designs.
  • Document tech decisions so others can learn.
  • Keep an experimental mindset — try ideas, measure, and improve.

How I work with clients & collaborators

Typical engagements are short — a week to a month for simple sites or prototypes, and longer for more complex apps. I break work into milestones, maintain regular updates, and always ask for feedback at every step. For student collaborators, I provide mentorship on architecture and deployment.

Case Studies — In depth

Detailed breakdown of three projects — goals, process, and measurable results.

Campus Event Portal

Duration: 3 weeks • Team: Solo • Role: Full stack developer

Problem: College technical club needed a way to manage event registrations and on-ground check-ins without spreadsheets. Solution: A simple portal where organisers create events, users RSVP, and check-in via QR codes. I used Firebase Auth, Firestore and hosted on Netlify.

Registrations ? 3x
Check-in time ? 80%
Workload ? 70%

Key lessons: Keep scope tight, focus on core flows, and validate with real users early.

Study Buddy App

Duration: 6 weeks • Team: 2 • Role: Lead dev & designer

Problem: Students needed a way to coordinate study sessions and share notes. Solution: Offline-first Flutter app with simple group chat and note sharing. Designed UX for quick onboarding and created an analytics dashboard to measure retention.

Retention (7-day) 42%
Peer sign-ups 120+

Voice Search Demo

Duration: 1 week • Team: Solo • Role: Prototype

Problem: Demonstrate modern web capabilities in a class presentation. Solution: Implemented a client-side voice search using the Web Speech API and integrated text-to-speech responses for results. Result: Helpful demo that showcased instant voice interactions without backend changes.

Demo success rate 100%
Works in Chrome & Edge
